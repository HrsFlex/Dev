What is sun storm ?

This is the core of the Sun, where nuclear fusion reactions take place continuously. Because of these reactions, charged particles are always moving, and the flow of these charged particles creates electric current. In physics, we know that the presence of electric current creates a magnetic field. ...

In the beginning, the magnetic field lines of the Sun also travel from North to South, but due to the Sun's immense gravity, these magnetic lines twist and stretch within themselves, forming knots. These knots trap matter and create dark patches, which we scientifically call Sunspots. We can capture these Sunspots even with basic telescopes.

Due to the cooling and condensing of the trapped matter, it turns into a plasma cloud. This plasma then rains down onto the surface of the Sun. However, sometimes, due to some disturbances, a part of the plasma separates from the Sun's magnetic field. These Sunspots, like a gun, shoot magnetized plasma clouds at the speed of light into the solar system. This only happens when the Sun reorganizes its magnetic field and changes its polarity every 11 years.

These solar flares spread throughout the solar system. Earth, being a habitable planet, feels their effects the most. The last time these solar flares hit was in 2014, and they might hit again in 2025. These flares can cause significant damage, and I’m even talking about massive destruction.
